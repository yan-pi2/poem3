The Library

"It looks like any building we pass along in a street."

What do these lines mean?

The library might appear like an ordinary building from the outside.

"Made of stone, glass, marble, iron, and concrete."

What do these lines mean?

Its structure is made from common materials like stone and concrete.

"But once you pass through the door, you are inside a world."

What do these lines mean?

Once you enter, you are transported to an entirely different world filled with knowledge.

"A world beyond your imagination, unlike any place you've been."

What do these lines mean?

The library offers an extraordinary experience, different from any other location.

The Quiet Life

"A happy man has few simple wants."

What do these lines mean?

A happy person lives a content life with only basic needs.

"He breathes his native air and enjoys his own ground."

What do these lines mean?

He enjoys living on the land where he was born.

"His house is small, his friends are few, and his desires are fewer."

What do these lines mean?

A happy person leads a simple life with limited friends and minimal desires.

"But his heart is light, and his joy is pure."

What do these lines mean?

His happiness is genuine, and he feels free and lighthearted.

The Road Not Taken

"Two roads diverged in a yellow wood, and sorry I could not travel both."

What do these lines mean?

The poet finds two paths in a forest but can only choose one.

"I took the one less traveled by, and that has made all the difference."

What do these lines mean?

Choosing the unconventional path greatly impacted his life.

"I doubted if I should ever come back."

What do these lines mean?

The poet wonders if he will ever return to explore the other road.

Windy Nights

"Whenever the moon and stars are set, whenever the wind is high."

What do these lines mean?

The poet describes dark, windy nights when the moon and stars are hidden.

"All night long in the dark and wet, a man goes riding by."

What do these lines mean?

A mysterious rider travels through the stormy night.

"And the trees and the houses dark and grim."

What do these lines mean?

The surroundings, such as trees and houses, appear eerie and shadowy.
